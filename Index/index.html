Tips for Financial Cybersecurity

Securing Your Finacial Future


Password Security & Securing Passwords

Password security is often overlooked, passwords tend to end up being something related to your personal life. Like a pets name followed by your birth year. This makes them easy to remember, but easier for intruders compromise. Creating randomly generated passwords that include uppercase, lowercase, numerical and special characters is an easy way to increase security and the longer the better. But this makes remembering them close to impossible. This is where securing your passwords with an AES-256 encrypted password manager comes in. Password managers are a great way to store multiple passwords. While only having to remember a single password. Think of it as a cyber safe!

Enabling Multifactor Authentication (MFA)

Multi-factor Authentication or MFA, is an additional shield for your account. When MFA is enabled, you require two or more types of virtual evidence. This normally comes in the form a code. The code can be accessed through a authentication application or sent directly to the mobile number associated to the account. It can be thought as an additional shield(s) to your account. You can set up more than one authentication type.

Use Encryption For Sensitive Data

Encryption is like a blender for your data. Your data goes in and the shredded data comes out. The data is not lost, it just comes out in a new form. To return the data back to its original form, you need a special key. In the case of our blended data, it requires a special glue to piece back together correctly. Without that glue the data is unreadable. Only one bottle of this special glue exists. AES-256 Encryption, currently the most secure form of encryption widely available. If you truly value your sensitive data, make sure you are using websites and applications that use AES-256 encryption for your sensitive data. To this day, it has not been broken. But always keep up-to-date with newer more secure methods.

Keep Your Applications & Systems Up-To-Date

This is one of the easiest ways to reduce the potential of threats and attacks against your information. Keep your applications and systems updated. You will be protected against exploits that have been exposed. For example, if you use Windows 11. You should regularly check and install security related updates. If you use Apple products, make sure you are using the most recent iOS version. Having out of date software can increase the exposure of your data. Most systems allow you to set updates to be automatic, even while you sleep. Do not neglect this feature!

Use Your Own Devices & Private Networks

Never! Access your accounts on shared devices or public networks. Even if your are using a friends device. You can never be sure what they have or have not done to expose their device(s) to potential threats and exploits. Public networks often have extremely weak security in place to allow easy access when connecting your devices. If you are just browsing news articles, go for it. But to avoid any exposure of sensitive data, do not access your financial accounts on public networks!
